# Form 2 Science Content Schema

## Purpose

This document defines a platform-ready `Form 2 Science` curriculum structure for an AI-first, tutor-supported tuition system aligned to Malaysia lower secondary KSSM learning.

It is designed to support:

- stronger experiment reasoning
- clearer scientific communication
- nutrition, biodiversity, and ecosystem understanding
- force, energy, and electricity foundations
- environmental and technology decision-making

This is a product blueprint, not a textbook chapter list.

## Scope

Subject:

- Science

Level:

- Form 2

Primary instructional model:

- AI handles daily concept reinforcement, guided explanation, structured practice, and revision sequencing
- Human tutors step in for experimental reasoning repair, difficult concept clarification, and intervention planning

|---|---|---|

## 3. Mastery Design

Each topic is broken into `4` mastery nodes:

1. `Recognition`
2. `Procedure`
3. `Procedure / transfer`
4. `Application`

This keeps Form 2 Science aligned with the same curriculum engine already used in Mathematics, English, Bahasa Melayu, and Science Form 1.

### Example: `A1 Experimental Design and Data Interpretation`

- `SCI2I1-01` Recognise investigation aims and variables
- `SCI2I1-02` Set up a fairer test with valid data points
- `SCI2I1-03` Interpret patterns from basic data
- `SCI2I1-04` Write a conclusion tied to evidence

### Example: `B1 Nutrition and Human Health`

- `SCI2L1-01` Recognise key food classes and health needs
- `SCI2L1-02` Relate nutrition choices to body needs
- `SCI2L1-03` Describe the digestion pathway simply
- `SCI2L1-04` Evaluate a simple health choice scientifically

### Example: `C3 Electricity and Simple Circuits`

- `SCI2P3-01` Recognise circuit components and symbols
- `SCI2P3-02` Describe current flow in a simple circuit
- `SCI2P3-03` Predict what happens when a circuit changes
- `SCI2P3-04` Explain a simple electrical setup safely

## 4. Recommended Learning Flow

For daily study, the AI tutor should sequence Form 2 Science like this:

1. Short observation or scenario warm-up
2. Micro lesson on the current mastery node
3. Guided interpretation task
4. Short explanation or decision check
5. Revision recommendation
6. Tutor intervention if misconception patterns repeat

### AI tutor should prioritise:

- evidence before conclusion
- observation before abstract explanation
- causal reasoning before memorised phrases
- decision-making with science reasons, not opinion alone

---

## 5. Common Misconception Families

The current Form 2 blueprint seeds these high-value misconception groups:

- pattern seen but conclusion overstated
- diagram neat but science language vague
- healthy choice without nutrient reasoning
- classification based on one weak trait
- ecosystem change seen as one-step only
- force named without motion link
- energy transfer listed without useful output
- circuit parts known but current path unclear
- Earth resources recalled without process understanding
- technology choice judged by preference only

These are designed to drive:

- AI feedback wording
- tutor intervention rules
- parent-facing progress summaries

---

## 6. Tutor Intervention Points

Tutors should be alerted when students show patterns like:

- designing experiments without keeping evidence and conclusion aligned
- describing observations with weak science vocabulary
- making health choices without nutrient-based reasoning
- tracing only one effect in an ecosystem change
- confusing force, energy, and motion roles
- naming electrical parts without understanding the complete current path
- making environmental recommendations without scientific trade-off reasoning

Tutor actions inside the platform should include:

- assign a narrower misconception repair path
- reteach one causal chain with a familiar example
- compare two answers and justify which is more scientific
- leave a parent-visible note about reasoning support needed

---

## 7. Parent Visibility

For Form 2 Science, parent-facing progress should highlight:

- current concept focus
- explanation accuracy
- practical reasoning
- decision-making quality
- next tutor-led revision priority

### Example parent summary

- Current focus: `Electricity and Simple Circuits`
- Improved this week: `Experimental Design and Data Interpretation`
- Needs support: `Energy, Work and Efficiency`
- Next step: `Explain useful output and wasted energy more clearly`

## 8. MVP Build Order

Recommended build order for authored Form 2 Science content:

1. `A1` Experimental Design and Data Interpretation
2. `B1` Nutrition and Human Health
3. `C1` Force and Motion Basics
4. `C3` Electricity and Simple Circuits
5. `D2` Environmental Responsibility and Applied Technology
6. `A2` Observation, Models and Scientific Communication
7. `B3` Dynamic Ecosystems and Balance
8. `C2` Energy, Work and Efficiency
9. `B2` Biodiversity and Classification
10. `D1` Earth Processes, Resources and Space Awareness

This order gets the platform to a useful Form 2 Science core first:

- experiment reasoning
- health and living systems
- force and electricity understanding
- science-based decision making

before widening into all Earth and biodiversity layers.

---

## 9. Summary

`Form 2 Science` in this platform should not become a loose set of facts, diagrams, and practical worksheets.

It should behave like a structured mastery system:

- 4 domains
- 10 topics
- 40 mastery nodes
- linked misconception rules
- tutor intervention hooks
- parent-visible progress language

This lets Science continue as a coherent curriculum engine beyond Form 1.

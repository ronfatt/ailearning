# Form 5 English Content Schema

## Purpose

This document defines a platform-ready `Form 5 English` curriculum structure for an AI-first, tutor-supported tuition system aligned to Malaysia upper secondary and SPM-oriented learning needs.

It extends the earlier English pathway into stronger:

- cross-text synthesis and evaluation
- tone, perspective, and intention analysis
- advanced editing and reformulation
- register-sensitive vocabulary and voice control
- discursive essay writing
- directed and exam-task writing

This is a product blueprint, not a textbook chapter list.

## Scope

Subject:

- English

Level:

- Form 5

Primary instructional model:

- AI handles daily reading analysis, editing practice, sentence reformulation, and writing rehearsal
- Human tutors step in for critical interpretation, essay coaching, intervention planning, and SPM-style support

---

## 1. Domain Map

| Domain Code | Domain Name | Why It Exists |
|---|---|---|
| `A` | Reading, Interpretation and Evaluation | Build stronger synthesis, evaluation, and interpretive control for SPM-style comprehension work |
| `B` | Grammar, Accuracy and Reformulation | Improve accuracy, clarity, transformation control, and paragraph-level cohesion |
| `C` | Vocabulary, Register and Writer Effect | Develop nuance, connotation, voice, and audience-fit language decisions |
| `D` | Extended Writing and Exam Response | Prepare students for discursive essays, directed writing, and full exam-task response control |

## 3. Mastery Design

Each topic is broken into `4` mastery nodes:

1. `Recognition`
2. `Procedure`
3. `Procedure / transfer`
4. `Application`

This keeps Form 5 English aligned with the platform-wide mastery model already used in mathematics and earlier English levels.

### Example: `A1 Synthesis, Evaluation and Cross-Text Comparison`

- `E5R1-01` Recognise key claims across more than one text
- `E5R1-02` Combine evidence across texts accurately
- `E5R1-03` Compare and evaluate how ideas are presented
- `E5R1-04` Write a critical cross-text comparison

### Example: `B2 Transformation, Reformulation and Meaning Control`

- `E5G2-01` Recognise meaning-preserving transformation targets
- `E5G2-02` Transform structure while keeping meaning
- `E5G2-03` Reformulate for tone, emphasis, or task fit
- `E5G2-04` Use reformulation in exam-style response tasks

### Example: `D1 Discursive and Extended Essay Writing`

- `E5W1-01` Recognise strong discursive essay structure
- `E5W1-02` Plan an extended essay with clear reasoning
- `E5W1-03` Develop and connect essay paragraphs
- `E5W1-04` Write a sustained extended essay

## 4. Recommended Learning Flow

For daily study, the AI tutor should sequence Form 5 English like this:

1. Reading or editing warm-up
2. Micro lesson on the current mastery node
3. Guided practice
4. Short independent check
5. Revision recommendation
6. Tutor intervention if needed

### AI tutor should prioritise:

- synthesis before surface comparison
- evidence plus evaluation, not evidence alone
- clarity and nuance together in reformulation tasks
- essay planning before full discursive drafting
- audience and register control in exam-task writing

## 5. Common Misconception Families

The current Form 5 blueprint seeds these high-value misconception groups:

- `multi-text-summary-no-judgement`
- `tone-label-without-proof`
- `summary-and-opinion-collision`
- `grammar-fixed-clarity-ignored`
- `reformulation-loses-nuance`
- `connectors-mask-weak-logic`
- `figurative-answer-too-literal`
- `voice-does-not-fit-task`
- `essay-has-points-no-line`
- `task-complete-but-response-thin`

These are designed to drive:

- AI feedback wording
- tutor intervention rules
- parent-facing progress summaries

---

## 6. Tutor Intervention Points

Tutors should be alerted when students show patterns like:

- combining texts without actually evaluating them
- naming tone or intention without evidence
- mixing summary and opinion in the same line
- editing grammar while leaving weak clarity untouched
- transforming sentences but changing the original meaning
- using cohesive markers without real argument progression
- interpreting figurative language too literally
- writing understandable responses with the wrong voice or register
- producing essays with ideas but no sustained line of reasoning
- completing exam tasks mechanically without enough depth or adaptation

Tutor actions inside the platform should include:

- assign a narrow revision path
- model one stronger analytical explanation
- rewrite one paragraph for clarity and argument progression
- give essay-structure feedback
- leave a parent-visible support note

---

## 7. Parent Visibility

For Form 5 English, parent-facing progress should highlight:

- reading evaluation strength
- editing and clarity control
- essay structure and development
- weak communication area
- next focus

### Example parent summary

- Current focus: `Synthesis, Evaluation and Cross-Text Comparison`
- Improved this week: `Advanced Editing for Accuracy and Clarity`
- Needs support: `Essay line of reasoning`
- Next step: `Use evidence to compare and evaluate two text viewpoints`

## 8. MVP Build Order

Recommended build order for authored Form 5 English content:

1. `A1 Synthesis, Evaluation and Cross-Text Comparison`
2. `A2 Tone, Perspective and Writer Intention`
3. `B1 Advanced Editing for Accuracy and Clarity`
4. `B2 Transformation, Reformulation and Meaning Control`
5. `D1 Discursive and Extended Essay Writing`
6. `D2 Directed, Functional and Exam Task Writing`
7. `B3 Cohesion, Argument and Paragraph Control`
8. `C2 Register, Voice and Audience Awareness`
9. `A3 Summary Precision and Critical Response`
10. `C1 Nuance, Connotation and Figurative Interpretation`

This order gets the platform to a useful Form 5 core first:

- cross-text evaluation
- stronger editing and reformulation
- essay and exam-task control

before widening the full stylistic and nuance layer.

---

## 9. Summary

`Form 5 English` in this platform should not be a loose mix of essay prompts, grammar drills, and comprehension passages.

It should behave like a structured mastery system:

- 4 domains
- 10 topics
- 40 mastery nodes
- linked misconception rules
- tutor intervention hooks
- parent-visible progress language

This creates a clear upper secondary progression from:

- Form 1 foundations
- Form 2 control and coherence
- Form 3 analytical reading and extended response
- Form 4 synthesis and essay-ready structure
- into Form 5 evaluation, reformulation, and exam-fit communication

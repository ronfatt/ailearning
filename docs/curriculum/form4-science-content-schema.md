# Form 4 Science Content Schema

## Purpose

This document defines a platform-ready `Form 4 Science` curriculum structure for an AI-first, tutor-supported tuition system aligned to Malaysia upper secondary KSSM learning.

It is designed to support:

- stronger data reliability judgement and scientific justification
- better investigation design using variables and models
- deeper understanding of cell processes, health, reproduction, and continuity
- clearer explanation of material structure, chemical change, and electricity
- more mature Earth-system, climate, technology, and sustainability reasoning

This is a product blueprint, not a textbook chapter list.

## Scope

Subject:

- Science

Level:

- Form 4

Primary instructional model:

- AI handles daily explanation, structured practice, misconception repair, and revision sequencing
- Human tutors step in for multi-step reasoning repair, scientific writing support, and intervention planning

## 3. Mastery Design

Each topic is broken into `4` mastery nodes:

1. `Recognition`
2. `Procedure`
3. `Procedure / transfer`
4. `Application`

This keeps Form 4 Science aligned with the same curriculum engine already used in Mathematics, English, Bahasa Melayu, and lower secondary Science.

### Example: `A1 Data Reliability and Scientific Justification`

- `SCI4I1-01` Recognise reliable and weak evidence patterns
- `SCI4I1-02` Judge reliability in a simple data set
- `SCI4I1-03` Separate observation, trend and claim
- `SCI4I1-04` Justify a scientific claim with balanced evidence

### Example: `C2 Chemical Change and Reaction Evidence`

- `SCI4P2-01` Recognise signs of chemical change
- `SCI4P2-02` Compare physical and chemical changes
- `SCI4P2-03` Interpret simple reaction evidence
- `SCI4P2-04` Explain a reaction-based situation scientifically

### Example: `D2 Technology, Resources and Sustainable Decisions`

- `SCI4E2-01` Recognise resource trade-offs and technology roles
- `SCI4E2-02` Compare technology or resource options scientifically
- `SCI4E2-03` Trace short- and long-term effects of a decision
- `SCI4E2-04` Recommend a sustainable decision with trade-offs

## 4. Recommended Learning Flow

For daily study, the AI tutor should sequence Form 4 Science like this:

1. Scenario or evidence warm-up
2. Micro lesson on the current mastery node
3. Guided scientific explanation or comparison task
4. Short justification checkpoint
5. Revision recommendation
6. Tutor intervention if reasoning patterns repeat

### AI tutor should prioritise:

- evidence before certainty
- process links before memorised labels
- structure-property and cause-effect reasoning
- trade-offs and justification in sustainability topics

---

## 5. Common Misconception Families

The current Form 4 blueprint seeds these high-value misconception groups:

- claim stronger than evidence quality
- investigation improved without variable-control logic
- cell processes recalled without linked biological effect
- health prevention justified by rule, not biology
- genetics treated as a word list
- particle ideas not linked to material properties
- reaction evidence named without process explanation
- electricity or power use described without transfer chain
- climate patterns explained as isolated events
- sustainable choices given without trade-off thinking

These are designed to drive:

- AI feedback wording
- tutor intervention rules
- parent-facing progress summaries

---

## 6. Tutor Intervention Points

Tutors should be alerted when students show patterns like:

- strong conclusions with weak or incomplete evidence
- investigation redesign without explaining variable control
- naming biological processes without linking them to outcomes
- describing electricity or energy without tracing transfer paths
- talking about sustainability without discussing trade-offs

Tutor actions inside the platform should include:

- assign a narrower misconception repair path
- reteach one linked cause-effect chain with a familiar example
- compare two answers and justify which is more scientific
- leave a parent-visible note about reasoning support needed

## 7. Parent Visibility

For Form 4 Science, parent-facing progress should highlight:

- current concept focus
- reasoning quality
- evidence use
- explanation maturity
- next tutor-led revision priority

### Example parent summary

- Current focus: `Chemical Change and Reaction Evidence`
- Improved this week: `Data Reliability and Scientific Justification`
- Needs support: `Technology, Resources and Sustainable Decisions`
- Next step: `Use evidence and trade-offs when choosing a sustainable option`

## 8. MVP Build Order

Recommended build order for authored Form 4 Science content:

1. `A1` Data Reliability and Scientific Justification
2. `B1` Cell Processes and Transport
3. `C2` Chemical Change and Reaction Evidence
4. `D2` Technology, Resources and Sustainable Decisions
5. `A2` Models, Variables and Investigation Design
6. `B2` Human Health, Immunity and Disease Prevention
7. `C3` Energy Transfer, Electricity and Power Use
8. `C1` Atomic Structure and Material Properties
9. `B3` Reproduction, Genetics and Continuity
10. `D1` Earth Systems, Space and Climate Influence

This order gets the platform to a useful Form 4 Science core first:

- stronger evidence judgement
- biological and physical process explanation
- reaction and electricity reasoning
- sustainability decision-making

before widening across all upper secondary layers.

---

## 9. Summary

`Form 4 Science` in this platform should not become a loose set of reaction facts, biology notes, and climate examples.

It should behave like a structured mastery system:

- 4 domains
- 10 topics
- 40 mastery nodes
- linked misconception rules
- tutor intervention hooks
- parent-visible progress language

This lets Science continue as a coherent curriculum engine from lower secondary into upper secondary.

# Form 3 Science Content Schema

## Purpose

This document defines a platform-ready `Form 3 Science` curriculum structure for an AI-first, tutor-supported tuition system aligned to Malaysia lower secondary KSSM learning.

It is designed to support:

- stronger evidence judgement and investigation reasoning
- systems thinking and model-based explanation
- coordination, reproduction, and adaptation in living things
- density, pressure, waves, and magnetism reasoning
- climate, Earth change, and sustainability decisions

This is a product blueprint, not a textbook chapter list.

## Scope

Subject:

- Science

Level:

- Form 3

Primary instructional model:

- AI handles daily explanation, structured practice, misconception repair, and revision sequencing
- Human tutors step in for multi-step reasoning repair, scientific explanation coaching, and intervention planning

## 3. Mastery Design

Each topic is broken into `4` mastery nodes:

1. `Recognition`
2. `Procedure`
3. `Procedure / transfer`
4. `Application`

This keeps Form 3 Science aligned with the same curriculum engine already used in Mathematics, English, Bahasa Melayu, and Science Form 1–2.

### Example: `A1 Evidence Quality and Experimental Reasoning`

- `SCI3I1-01` Recognise stronger and weaker scientific evidence
- `SCI3I1-02` Improve a basic investigation design
- `SCI3I1-03` Interpret patterns and limits in data
- `SCI3I1-04` Justify a conclusion with balanced evidence

### Example: `B3 Adaptation, Survival and Population Change`

- `SCI3L3-01` Recognise adaptation and survival pressures
- `SCI3L3-02` Relate traits to survival advantage
- `SCI3L3-03` Trace how conditions affect populations
- `SCI3L3-04` Explain a survival or adaptation case scientifically

### Example: `D2 Human Impact, Resources and Sustainable Solutions`

- `SCI3E2-01` Recognise resource pressure and human impact
- `SCI3E2-02` Compare resource choices using science criteria
- `SCI3E2-03` Trace consequences of human decisions
- `SCI3E2-04` Recommend a sustainable solution with evidence

## 4. Recommended Learning Flow

For daily study, the AI tutor should sequence Form 3 Science like this:

1. Brief observation, scenario, or evidence check
2. Micro lesson on the current mastery node
3. Guided scientific explanation task
4. Short compare-or-justify checkpoint
5. Revision recommendation
6. Tutor intervention if reasoning patterns repeat

### AI tutor should prioritise:

- evidence before opinion
- linked cause-effect before memorised statements
- systems reasoning before isolated facts
- trade-offs and justification in sustainability topics

---

## 5. Common Misconception Families

The current Form 3 blueprint seeds these high-value misconception groups:

- better data suggested without explaining why it improves evidence
- model copied as a picture, not used as an explanation tool
- response described without a clear control pathway
- variation listed without biological meaning
- adaptation confused with short-term choice
- pressure discussed without force-area logic
- wave vocabulary memorised without transfer understanding
- magnetism known without linking to device function
- climate and weather merged together
- sustainability answers staying at slogan level

These are designed to drive:

- AI feedback wording
- tutor intervention rules
- parent-facing progress summaries

---

## 6. Tutor Intervention Points

Tutors should be alerted when students show patterns like:

- improving an investigation without explaining why evidence becomes stronger
- using models without showing system relationships
- naming body responses without tracing coordination pathways
- describing adaptation without linking it to survival and environment
- confusing pressure, density, and material behaviour
- using wave terms without explaining what transfers
- giving sustainability answers without evidence or trade-offs

Tutor actions inside the platform should include:

- assign a narrower misconception repair path
- reteach one cause-effect chain with a familiar example
- contrast two explanations and justify which is more scientific
- leave a parent-visible note about reasoning support needed

## 7. Parent Visibility

For Form 3 Science, parent-facing progress should highlight:

- current concept focus
- explanation quality
- evidence use
- scientific reasoning maturity
- next tutor-led revision priority

### Example parent summary

- Current focus: `Density, Pressure and Material Behaviour`
- Improved this week: `Evidence Quality and Experimental Reasoning`
- Needs support: `Human Impact, Resources and Sustainable Solutions`
- Next step: `Use evidence and trade-offs when recommending a sustainability action`

7. `C2` Sound, Waves and Signal Transfer
8. `C3` Magnetism and Electromagnetic Applications
9. `B2` Reproduction, Growth and Variation
10. `D1` Climate Systems and Earth Change

This order gets the platform to a useful Form 3 Science core first:

- stronger reasoning and evidence judgement
- life-system explanations
- physical science cause-effect thinking
- sustainability decision-making

before widening across all Earth and communication layers.

---

## 9. Summary

`Form 3 Science` in this platform should not become a loose collection of facts and diagrams.

It should behave like a structured mastery system:

- 4 domains
- 10 topics
- 40 mastery nodes
- linked misconception rules
- tutor intervention hooks
- parent-visible progress language

This lets Science continue as a coherent curriculum engine from Form 1 to Form 3.

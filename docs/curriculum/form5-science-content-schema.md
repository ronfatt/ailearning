# Form 5 Science Content Schema

## Purpose

This document defines a platform-ready `Form 5 Science` curriculum structure for an AI-first, tutor-supported tuition system aligned to Malaysia upper secondary KSSM learning.

It is designed to support:

- stronger evidence evaluation and scientific argument
- better judgement of investigation quality and decision making
- deeper explanation of homeostasis, inheritance, and health science
- clearer reasoning about reaction systems, force-energy links, and technology use
- more mature climate, sustainability, and future-focused science decisions

This is a product blueprint, not a textbook chapter list.

## Scope

Subject:

- Science

Level:

- Form 5

Primary instructional model:

- AI handles daily explanation, structured practice, misconception repair, and revision sequencing
- Human tutors step in for extended reasoning repair, scenario interpretation, and intervention planning

## 3. Mastery Design

Each topic is broken into `4` mastery nodes:

1. `Recognition`
2. `Procedure`
3. `Procedure / transfer`
4. `Application`

This keeps Form 5 Science aligned with the same curriculum engine used across Mathematics, English, Bahasa Melayu, and lower-secondary-to-upper-secondary Science.

### Example: `A1 Evidence Evaluation and Scientific Argument`

- `SCI5I1-01` Recognise strong and weak scientific arguments
- `SCI5I1-02` Compare evidence quality across sources
- `SCI5I1-03` Separate claim, support and limitation
- `SCI5I1-04` Build a balanced scientific argument

### Example: `C2 Force, Energy and Motion in Systems`

- `SCI5P2-01` Recognise linked force and energy behaviour
- `SCI5P2-02` Trace energy transfer through motion
- `SCI5P2-03` Compare system performance and efficiency
- `SCI5P2-04` Explain a force-energy system scenario

### Example: `D2 Resources, Innovation and Sustainable Futures`

- `SCI5E2-01` Recognise resource and innovation trade-offs
- `SCI5E2-02` Compare future options using science criteria
- `SCI5E2-03` Trace short- and long-term sustainability effects
- `SCI5E2-04` Recommend a future-facing sustainable pathway

## 4. Recommended Learning Flow

For daily study, the AI tutor should sequence Form 5 Science like this:

1. Evidence or scenario warm-up
2. Micro lesson on the current mastery node
3. Guided scientific comparison or explanation task
4. Justification checkpoint
5. Revision recommendation
6. Tutor intervention if reasoning patterns repeat

### AI tutor should prioritise:

- evidence before certainty
- systems and trade-offs before memorised labels
- scientific mechanism before recommendation
- balanced judgement in health, climate, and technology questions

---

## 5. Common Misconception Families

The current Form 5 blueprint seeds these high-value misconception groups:

- argument stated without balancing evidence
- decision made without checking investigation quality
- homeostasis described as a single event
- variation mentioned without continuity logic
- health recommendation not backed by biology
- reaction or material change explained only by surface signs
- force or energy mentioned without system links
- technology judged by benefit only
- climate change treated as single-cause
- future sustainability solution given without trade-off thinking

These are designed to drive:

- AI feedback wording
- tutor intervention rules
- parent-facing progress summaries

---

## 6. Tutor Intervention Points

Tutors should be alerted when students show patterns like:

- strong claims with weak or unbalanced evidence
- choosing between science options without quality checks
- explaining biological regulation without feedback logic
- describing technology benefits without risk or limitation
- talking about sustainability futures without trade-offs

Tutor actions inside the platform should include:

- narrow the misconception repair path
- compare two arguments and justify which is scientifically stronger
- reteach one linked cause-effect chain with a grounded example
- leave a parent-visible note about reasoning maturity or intervention need

---

## 7. Parent Visibility

For Form 5 Science, parent-facing progress should highlight:

- current concept focus
- evidence quality and reasoning maturity
- explanation strength
- decision-making quality
- next tutor-led revision priority

### Example parent summary

- Current focus: `Force, Energy and Motion in Systems`
- Improved this week: `Evidence Evaluation and Scientific Argument`
- Needs support: `Resources, Innovation and Sustainable Futures`
- Next step: `Use evidence and trade-offs to defend a future-facing science decision`

## 9. Summary

`Form 5 Science` in this platform should not become a loose mix of climate opinions, reaction facts, and health reminders.

It should behave like a structured mastery system:

- 4 domains
- 10 topics
- 40 mastery nodes
- linked misconception rules
- tutor intervention hooks
- parent-visible progress language

This lets Science reach a coherent Form 1–5 curriculum engine with upper-secondary reasoning and decision-making built in.

# Form 1 Science Content Schema

## Purpose

This document defines a platform-ready `Form 1 Science` curriculum structure for an AI-first, tutor-supported tuition system aligned to Malaysia lower secondary KSSM learning.

It is designed to support:

- scientific inquiry habits
- practical reasoning
- life science foundations
- matter, energy, and light concepts
- earth systems and sustainability thinking

This is a product blueprint, not a textbook chapter list.

## Scope

Subject:

- Science

Level:

- Form 1

Primary instructional model:

- AI handles daily concept explanation, structured practice, observation interpretation, and revision sequencing
- Human tutors step in for practical clarification, misconception repair, reasoning support, and intervention planning

---

## 1. Domain Map

| Domain Code | Domain Name | Why It Exists |
|---|---|---|
| `A` | Scientific Inquiry and Laboratory Skills | Build variable control, observation habits, apparatus use, and safe practical routines |
| `B` | Life Processes and Living Systems | Establish cell knowledge, plant and animal processes, and ecosystem relationships |
| `C` | Matter, Energy and Physical Interactions | Give students a workable model for matter, heat, and light in everyday situations |
| `D` | Earth, Environment and Applied Science | Connect weather, water, resources, technology, and sustainability with scientific reasoning |

| Topic Code | Topic Name | Summary |

## 3. Mastery Design

Each topic is broken into `4` mastery nodes:

1. `Recognition`
2. `Procedure`
3. `Procedure / transfer`
4. `Application`

This keeps Form 1 Science aligned with the same curriculum engine already used in Mathematics, English, and Bahasa Melayu.

### Example: `A1 Scientific Investigation and Variables`

- `SCI1I1-01` Recognise questions, observations and variables
- `SCI1I1-02` Identify manipulated, responding and constant variables
- `SCI1I1-03` Plan a simple fair test
- `SCI1I1-04` Draw a justified conclusion from evidence

### Example: `B1 Cell Structure and Organisation`

- `SCI1L1-01` Recognise basic cell structures
- `SCI1L1-02` Match structures to functions
- `SCI1L1-03` Relate cells to tissues and organs
- `SCI1L1-04` Explain how structure supports a living system

### Example: `C1 States of Matter and Particle Arrangement`

- `SCI1P1-01` Recognise solids, liquids and gases
- `SCI1P1-02` Describe particle arrangement and movement
- `SCI1P1-03` Relate heating and cooling to changes of state
- `SCI1P1-04` Use the particle model to explain an everyday phenomenon

## 4. Recommended Learning Flow

For daily study, the AI tutor should sequence Form 1 Science like this:

1. Quick observation or concept warm-up
2. Micro lesson on one current mastery node
3. Guided explanation with one worked example
4. Short interpretation or classification task
5. Revision recommendation
6. Tutor escalation if misconception patterns repeat

### AI tutor should prioritise:

- observation before explanation
- fair-test thinking before memorised experiment steps
- process relationships before isolated definitions
- cause-effect reasoning before long written responses

---

## 5. Common Misconception Families

The current Form 1 blueprint seeds these high-value misconception groups:

- variables identified but not controlled
- apparatus chosen but units wrong
- cell parts memorised without function
- photosynthesis and respiration merged together
- food chains treated as a single fixed line
- particle model taken too literally
- heat confused with temperature
- reflection rays drawn by guess
- water cycle stages listed without links
- sustainability answers remain moral, not scientific

These are designed to drive:

- AI feedback wording
- tutor intervention rules
- parent-facing science progress summaries

---

## 6. Tutor Intervention Points

Tutors should be alerted when students show patterns like:

- naming variables without controlling them in planning
- measuring with the right tool but wrong unit or scale
- recalling science facts without linking them to process
- confusing plant food-making with energy release
- giving ecosystem answers that ignore interdependence
- treating heat and temperature as the same idea
- drawing reflection answers without using the normal and angles
- giving sustainability opinions without scientific cause-effect explanation

Tutor actions inside the platform should include:

- assign a mini practical reasoning path
- replay one concept with a concrete example
- reduce scope to one misconception family
- add a parent-visible note about reasoning support needed

## 7. Parent Visibility

For Form 1 Science, parent-facing progress should highlight:

- current science focus
- concept understanding
- practical reasoning
- explanation accuracy
- next tutor-led revision priority

### Example parent summary

- Current focus: `States of Matter and Particle Arrangement`
- Improved this week: `Scientific Investigation and Variables`
- Needs support: `Heat and Thermal Change`
- Next step: `Use particle ideas to explain daily changes of state`

## 8. MVP Build Order

Recommended build order for authored Form 1 Science content:

1. `A1` Scientific Investigation and Variables
2. `B1` Cell Structure and Organisation
3. `C1` States of Matter and Particle Arrangement
4. `C2` Heat and Thermal Change
5. `D1` Earth, Water and Weather Systems
6. `B3` Ecosystems and Interdependence
7. `A2` Measurement, Apparatus and Lab Safety
8. `B2` Respiration and Photosynthesis Foundations
9. `C3` Light, Reflection and Basic Optics
10. `D2` Resources, Sustainability and Simple Technology

This order gets the platform to a useful Form 1 Science core first:

- inquiry habits
- foundational life science
- matter and energy concepts
- explanation of familiar phenomena

before widening to all environment and applied-science layers.

---

## 9. Summary

`Form 1 Science` in this platform should not become a loose bank of experiments, definitions, and multiple-choice facts.

It should behave like a structured mastery system:

- 4 domains
- 10 topics
- 40 mastery nodes
- linked misconception rules
- tutor intervention hooks
- parent-visible progress language

This lets Science join the same curriculum engine already powering Mathematics, English, and Bahasa Melayu.

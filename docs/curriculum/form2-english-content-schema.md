# Form 2 English Content Schema

## Purpose

This document defines a platform-ready `Form 2 English` curriculum structure for an AI-first, tutor-supported tuition system aligned to Malaysia lower secondary learning.

It extends the Form 1 English foundation into stronger:

- comparison reading
- inference with tone and viewpoint
- grammar control across connected writing
- vocabulary precision
- paragraph coherence
- guided and functional writing

This is a product blueprint, not a textbook chapter list.

## Scope

Subject:

- English

Level:

- Form 2

Primary instructional model:

- AI handles daily reading interpretation, grammar drills, vocabulary practice, and guided writing support
- Human tutors step in for explanation, revision planning, confidence coaching, and writing intervention

---

## 1. Domain Map

| Domain Code | Domain Name | Why It Exists |
|---|---|---|
| `A` | Reading and Interpretation | Move students from literal reading into comparison, tone, point of view, and text organisation |
| `B` | Grammar and Sentence Control | Strengthen tense consistency, functional grammar, clauses, and sentence variety |
| `C` | Vocabulary and Meaning Precision | Improve lexical choice, context accuracy, and word-family understanding |
| `D` | Paragraph and Guided Writing | Build more coherent paragraphs and stronger fit-for-purpose guided writing |

---

## 2. Topic Register

| Topic Code | Topic Name | Summary |
|---|---|---|
| `A1` | Compare Main Ideas and Supporting Details | Compare key ideas and supporting information across short related texts |
| `A2` | Inference, Tone and Point of View | Infer meaning, tone, and writer or speaker attitude using evidence |
| `A3` | Reference, Sequence and Text Organisation | Track references, sequence events, and read text organisation more clearly |
| `B1` | Tense Consistency and Sentence Control | Maintain accuracy and tense control across linked writing |
| `B2` | Modals, Questions and Functional Grammar | Use modals, question forms, and practical grammar choices more accurately |
| `B3` | Clauses, Connectors and Sentence Variety | Use clauses and connectors to improve variety, flow, and precision |
| `C1` | Vocabulary Precision in Context | Choose more accurate meanings and replace vague vocabulary in context |
| `C2` | Word Formation and Lexical Relationships | Use prefixes, suffixes, word families, and lexical relationships more confidently |
| `D1` | Paragraph Development and Coherence | Develop paragraphs with stronger support, sequence, and coherence |
| `D2` | Guided Short Responses and Functional Writing | Produce clearer guided and practical writing that fits audience and purpose |

## 3. Mastery Design

Each topic is broken into `4` mastery nodes:

1. `Recognition`
2. `Procedure`
3. `Procedure / transfer`
4. `Application`

This keeps English aligned with the platform-wide mastery model already used in mathematics and English Form 1.

### Example: `A2 Inference, Tone and Point of View`

- `E2R2-01` Recognise tone and point of view signals
- `E2R2-02` Infer feeling, attitude, or intention
- `E2R2-03` Support interpretation with evidence
- `E2R2-04` Explain tone and point of view in a short response

### Example: `B3 Clauses, Connectors and Sentence Variety`

- `E2G3-01` Recognise clause and connector patterns
- `E2G3-02` Combine ideas using suitable connectors
- `E2G3-03` Create sentence variety with clause control
- `E2G3-04` Revise writing for variety and flow

### Example: `D1 Paragraph Development and Coherence`

- `E2W1-01` Recognise stronger paragraph organisation
- `E2W1-02` Expand support within a paragraph
- `E2W1-03` Use coherence devices to improve flow
- `E2W1-04` Write a coherent supported paragraph

---

## 4. Recommended Learning Flow

For daily study, the AI tutor should sequence Form 2 English like this:

1. Short reading or grammar warm-up
2. Micro lesson on the current node
3. Guided practice
4. Independent short check
5. Revision recommendation
6. Tutor intervention if stuck

### AI tutor should prioritise:

- evidence-based reading responses
- paragraph-level grammar control, not isolated drills only
- vocabulary precision, not just recognition
- writing for purpose and coherence, not only sentence correctness

---

## 5. Common Misconception Families

The current Form 2 blueprint seeds these high-value misconception groups:

- `comparison-without-contrast`
- `tone-as-topic-confusion`
- `sequence-order-loss`
- `paragraph-level-tense-drift`
- `function-form-mismatch`
- `connector-overload`
- `almost-right-vocabulary`
- `word-family-form-error`
- `support-without-link`
- `task-fit-but-thin-content`

These are designed to power:

- AI feedback wording
- tutor review and intervention
- parent-facing weak-area summaries

---

## 6. Tutor Intervention Points

Tutors should be alerted when students show patterns like:

- inference without supporting evidence
- weak understanding of tone or point of view
- tense drift across paragraph writing
- overuse or misuse of connectors
- precise vocabulary weakness despite understanding the general meaning
- guided writing that fits the format but lacks enough content

Tutor actions inside the platform should include:

- assign mini revision path
- unlock remedial grammar or reading support
- review one paragraph and model a stronger version
- leave a parent-visible support note

---

## 7. Parent Visibility

For Form 2 English, parent-facing progress should highlight:

- reading interpretation
- grammar stability in writing
- writing coherence
- weak language area
- next focus

### Example parent summary

- Current focus: `Inference, Tone and Point of View`
- Improved this week: `Compare Main Ideas and Supporting Details`
- Needs support: `Paragraph coherence and support`
- Next step: `Use evidence to explain writer attitude clearly`

## 8. MVP Build Order

Recommended build order for authored Form 2 English content:

1. `A2 Inference, Tone and Point of View`
2. `B1 Tense Consistency and Sentence Control`
3. `B3 Clauses, Connectors and Sentence Variety`
4. `D1 Paragraph Development and Coherence`
5. `D2 Guided Short Responses and Functional Writing`
6. `A1 Compare Main Ideas and Supporting Details`
7. `C1 Vocabulary Precision in Context`
8. `A3 Reference, Sequence and Text Organisation`
9. `C2 Word Formation and Lexical Relationships`
10. `B2 Modals, Questions and Functional Grammar`

This order gets the platform to a stronger usable core first:

- reading interpretation
- paragraph grammar control
- coherent guided writing

before filling out the rest of the language support layer.

---

## 9. Summary

`Form 2 English` in this platform should not be a flat set of grammar exercises.

It should behave like a structured mastery system:

- 4 domains
- 10 topics
- 40 mastery nodes
- linked misconception rules
- tutor intervention hooks
- parent-visible progress language

This creates a clean progression from Form 1 English into stronger lower secondary reading, writing, and language performance.
